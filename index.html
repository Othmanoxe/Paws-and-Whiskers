What we do:

Paws & Whiskers actively seek out and rescue vulnerable felines from the city streets, saving them from peril and uncertainty. Upon arrival, each cat undergoes comprehensive medical care to address immediate health concerns and ensure their well-being.

These shelters offer a safe haven, providing the cats with warmth, food, and love. Dedicated staff and volunteers work tirelessly to understand each cat's unique personality and needs. The ultimate goal is to match them with compatible families, guaranteeing a lifetime of love and care through adoption.

Why adopt a cat ?

Adopting a cat provides a loving home to a needy soul and brings boundless joy to your life. Their presence offers therapeutic comfort and an unmatched companionship.

In saving a feline life, you're gifting yourself unconditional love and silent understanding. Every whisker twitch and nuzzle serves as a reminder that love can be rescued and shared.

Get a chance to work with cats

During a Shelter Sleepover, you'll dive deep into the enchanting nocturnal world of our feline residents. Imagine yourself nestled amidst them in spaces that feel just like a cozy home, getting to know their unique nighttime quirks. You can read to them, engage in some gentle play, or perhaps just lie beside them, soaking in the soothing rhythm of their purring. This is especially significant for our shy or previously traumatized cats; your presence helps them build trust. Plus, your observations throughout the night offer invaluable insights, helping us tailor our approach for potential adopters. It's not just a sleepover; it's a stepping stone towards their forever home, and you're a vital part of that journey.

When you join in on Cat Yoga at our shelter, it's more than just a typical yoga session. Picture yourself flowing through poses with the gentle, curious interruptions of our feline companions. As you stretch into a downward dog, you might find a kitty playfully batting at your hair or curling up beside your mat during a meditative moment. These interactions not only deepen your connection to the cats but also add a unique, joyful element to your practice. The serene ambiance, combined with the cats' therapeutic presence, enhances relaxation and mindfulness. And remember, each stretch and purr is a step closer to finding these kitties their forever homes, with your participation making a world of difference.

Diving into Education and Outreach at our shelter, you're not just a volunteer; you become an ambassador for our feline friends. Imagine standing at community events or local schools, sharing stories of our whiskered residents, and enlightening others about the joys and responsibilities of pet ownership. As you interact with eager listeners, you'll be armed with materials, fun facts, and heartfelt anecdotes that showcase the profound impact of adoption. Your voice becomes the bridge between these cats and potential forever homes. Remember, every conversation you spark can change a life, potentially leading another cat to the warmth of a loving family. Your role in Education and Outreach is pivotal, transforming lives one chat at a time.

Engaging in Themed Adoption Days, you'll be at the heart of our most vibrant and joyful events. Imagine the shelter transformed with festive decorations, be it "Feline Fridays" or "Whisker Weekends", setting the stage for an unforgettable adoption experience. You'll play a key role, whether it's dressing up cats in cute outfits, sharing their individual tales with excited visitors, or organizing fun activities that bring their personalities to the forefront. Your enthusiasm and dedication set the tone, turning a regular adoption day into a memorable celebration. And with every playful theme and heartfelt interaction, you're not just creating moments of joy; you're crafting the beginning of countless forever stories.

Before Adopting:

How to plan and prepare for your new cat

Once you've decided to give a cat a home, it's time to get your home ready and prepare for a cat. You'll need to make sure you leave enough time to make adequate preparations, as well as to go shopping for everything they need. The most important thing your cat needs is a quiet, comfortable and secluded space of their or her own. It could be a spare bedroom or a cosy space in the corner of your living room. This will make sure your cat becomes familiar with one space before exploring the rest of your home.


Things you'll need for your new cat

  • One food bowl
  • One water bowl; remember to place your food and water bowl away from each other
  • A soft, warm and comfortable bed put somewhere quiet and safe
  • Access to a high spot where they can view their surroundings. A simple cardboard box should do the trick!
  • A litter tray, kept away from your cat’s food and water area
  • A sturdy scratching post
  • Remember: When providing beds, litter trays, scratching posts and food and water bowls, it is a good idea to provide one extra. When more than one cat is sharing the home, provide one of these items per cat plus one extra. For example, two cats should have access to at least three litter trays.



What does my cat need ?

It’s important to make sure your new cat has all the essentials they need before bringing them home. To help you provide for your pet, we’ve put together a checklist. Download it, take it shopping with you and stick it on your fridge – it’s got everything you need for your new addition.
